Overview

This is an expanded pan-cancer version of the immune-subtype driver analysis. For each of a large set of driver genes, we pool tumor samples across the cancer types in which the gene is recurrently point-mutated, and test whether the gene’s selection differs by immune subtype (Thorsson C1–C4, one-vs-rest), adjusting for age, sex, and tumor type of origin.

  • Genes: the 299 driver genes from Bailey et al. 2018 (Cell), filtered to those with a usable recurrently-mutated cohort (below).
  • Phenotype: immune subtype, one-vs-rest — C1 vs rest, C2 vs rest, C3 vs rest, C4 vs rest.
  • Covariates: age, sex, and cohort (tumor type). Sex is dropped for single-sex cohorts (e.g. UCEC/OV), and cohort is dropped for single-tumor cohorts.
  • Model: signature mode (BMRmode = "signature"), with the number of mutational signatures scaled to cohort size: k = 6 for ≤1000 samples, +1 per 200 samples above 1000, capped at 20.

Data sources

  • Mutations: TCGA PanCancer Atlas (32 studies) from cBioPortal, point mutations (SNVs) only, excluding hypermutators (>1000 mutations/sample).
  • Immune subtype (C1–C6): Thorsson et al. 2018 (Immunity) pan-immune table, matched by TCGA participant barcode.
  • Age, sex: cBioPortal clinical data.

Gene / cohort selection. Starting from the 299 Bailey drivers, for each gene we counted point mutations per tumor type and kept the tumor types with ≥5 point mutations; genes with a total of >20 point mutations were retained (254 genes). SKCM (melanoma) was excluded (its non-hypermutator subset gives a degenerate topic-model matrix). Each gene’s cohort is the union of its qualifying tumor types among the 27 tumor types with immune-subtype labels; genes sharing the same tumor set were grouped so the background mutation rate is estimated once per cohort. This yields 237 cohorts across 8,253 samples, run as 237 × 4 = 948 diffdriver analyses (BMR reused across the 4 subtypes).

Results are available for 220 genes (6 small low-mutation cohorts — EIF1AX, SOX17, PCBP1, IDH2, VHL, ELF3 — could not be fit in signature mode and are omitted).

Results

For each gene we take dd.p from its cohort run — the diffdriver likelihood-ratio test p-value (−2·(null − alt) log-likelihood, compared to χ² with 1 df). We then recompute FDR by Benjamini–Hochberg across all genes within each subtype (p.adjust(method = "fdr") applied separately to the ~220 p-values for C1, C2, C3, and C4). alpha_e is the subtype coefficient — positive = selected up in the subtype, negative = up in the rest. mut_freq is the cohort-level mutation frequency; mut.E1/mut.E0 are mutation counts in the subtype vs the rest.

Significant genes (dd.fdr < 0.1)

  • C2 vs rest (11): TP53 (up in C2), KRAS (up in rest), JAK2, COL5A1, CASP8, HLA-A, PPP2R1A, HLA-B, EZH2, RB1, FAT1 — all up in C2 except KRAS.
  • C3 vs rest (7): TP53, WHSC1/NSD2, FAT1, SMARCA4, BRCA1, LATS2, POLQ — all up in the rest.
  • C4 vs rest (2): CIC (up in rest), EGFR (up in C4).
  • C1 vs rest: none pass FDR 0.1.

The dominant, most consistent signal is TP53, which flips direction — enriched in C2 (“IFN-γ dominant”) but depleted (up in rest) for C3 (“inflammatory”) — consistent with the immune subtypes tracking distinct oncogenic-driver backgrounds. KRAS is depleted in C2. At the 0.1 threshold, C2 additionally picks up the antigen-presentation genes HLA-A and HLA-B, whose selection tracks immune-subtype differences in immune evasion. Notably EGFR is selected up in C4 (lymphocyte-depleted), and CIC in the rest of C4. This cohort-adjusted pan-cancer view (250 genes, 27 tumor types) extends the earlier tumor-type-restricted immune-subtype analyses.

Notes

  • FDR is recomputed within each subtype over the genes tested, so values differ from the per-tumor-type immune-subtype pages.
  • The full aggregated table (all 220 genes × 4 subtypes, with mutation frequency and cohort composition) is downloadable from the results table above.
